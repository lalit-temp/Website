Sikkim

Sikkim is a mountainous state located in the northeastern part of India. Nested in the foothills of the himalayas, it boasts some of the most serene scenaries in the country and in the world. It bordered by Nepal to the west, Bhutan to the east, Tibet to the north, and the Indian state of West Bengal to the south.

Sikkim is renowned for its spectacular mountainous terrain. The state is home to some of the world's highest peaks, including the majestic Kanchenjunga, the third-highest mountain on Earth. The Himalayan range dominates the skyline, providing a mesmerizing backdrop to every corner of the state.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Gangtok
  • Population: 610,577
  • Area: 2,740 sq mi
  • GDP: US$3.8 billion(2019)
